  • Posted on Anindita Chowdhary

I’ve known Bhashit for close to 3 years as a colleague at QOMPLX. In my opinion, Bhashit’s most appropriate job title would have been, “Principal Swiss Army Knife”. Let me explain.

Bhashit is someone who always keeps an eye on the big picture. He starts with the “what” and the “why” before the “how”. He is someone who will help you formulate it if it is not entirely clear before moving to the solutioning phase. His insightful questions and suggestions have helped give realistic shape to a broader vision on numerous occasions.

Once vision is set, he moves to iterative planning. He writes up the first draft of the “how”, presents it to the team and puts it up for critique/feedback/debate. He is a stickler for putting thought to paper so that it isn’t a moving target but flexible and open to debate the viability of the solution and make modifications as necessary. He communicates well and understands the strengths of individual team members to be able to delegate and get the work done.

He is someone who is passionate about continuous deployments to production. He championed a successful pilot program with his own team where every commit would have associated automated tests and be deployed to pre-prod and production environments; an idea which was radical given where we were as a company doing quarterly deployments. This model was later adopted by the rest of the teams.

Bhashit in the time I have known him has worn many hats in our Platform Engineering, SRE and Development teams. His singular goal is always to identify gaps and bring efficiency that he was successful at with every team he worked in. His additional strength lies in diving into unplanned work. He was critical to our production support initiatives at every level. Starting with helping teams identify metrics and create Grafana dashboards to troubleshooting critical development and platform issues that needed immediate fixes. The extra that he brought to the table was not to stop at fixing the issue but to put in place a post-mortem process that documents the RCA so that other team members can learn from it. He also hosted multiple self-initiated knowledge sharing sessions on diverse topics like - code quality, automated testing, metrics and dashboarding, API best practices, K8s for developers, troubleshooting Apache Flink,Kafka, Elasticsearch, documenting design etc.

All in all, Bhashit belongs to a rare species that is equally skilled as an individual contributor and a selfless visionary leader. If given a choice, I’d definitely want him on every team I work on in the future.
